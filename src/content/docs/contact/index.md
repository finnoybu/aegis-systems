## GitHub Discussions

The primary channel for all AEGIS™ community engagement is **GitHub Discussions** on the `aegis-initiative/aegis-constitution` repository.

[Open a Discussion →](https://github.com/aegis-initiative/aegis-constitution/discussions/categories/general)

---

## What to Use Discussions For

| Topic | Category to use |
|---|---|
| Questions about the constitution or its articles | General |
| Proposed amendments or RFC ideas | RFC Proposals |
| Implementation questions | Implementation |
| Reporting errors or inconsistencies | Documentation |
| Trademark or licensing inquiries | Legal & Licensing |
| Research collaborations or citations | Research |

---

## GitHub Issues

For specific, actionable items — errors in the text, broken links, or technical site issues — open an issue directly on the repository.

[Open an Issue →](https://github.com/aegis-initiative/aegis-constitution/issues)

---

## Contributing

The AEGIS™ Constitution is an open standard. Amendments are proposed through the RFC process — open a pull request with your proposed change and a rationale statement in the PR description. The amendment process is documented in the [Amendments](/constitution/amendments/) section.

All contributions must be made by humans. No amendment may be initiated, drafted, or approved by an AI system acting autonomously. Constitutional change is a human act.

---

## Response Time

This is a small initiative. Responses to discussions and issues are best-effort, typically within a few days. For urgent matters, note that in your message.
